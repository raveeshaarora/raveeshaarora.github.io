Projects

For the hackathon held at FINRA, my team created a stock market simulation app catered to millenials. The app contained of different stages corresponding to different securities but before a user would enter the next stage, they would have a small tutorial on what the security is, the risk involved, and what factors to consider when investing. We won the hackathon and got a smart watch as our prize! I worked on the UI of the app to make it appealing to the user and also figured how to connect the sign up page to the database in order to store accounts. We won first place for our app!

I created a lexer, parser, and interpreter using OCaml to pipeline a text file into a running SmallC program. SmallC is just a smaller version of the C language. This parser will be capable of parsing expressions, statements, and full programs. The parser operates on a token list assembled by my lexer and creates the correct statement corresponding to the input. The interpreter is able to interpret variables, int and bool types, equality and comparison operations, math and boolean operations, control flow, and printing. I used regular expressions and the typing patterns heavily integrated in OCaml to parse and interpret SmallC. In the end, I was able to convert small text files into running a program.
